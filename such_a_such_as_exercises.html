such a / such an + noun

Expressing intensity or surprise

Used before a singular countable noun (with adjective or alone) to intensify feeling or opinion.

It was such a great show!
She is such an amazing teacher.
He made such a long speech.
such as + examples

Giving examples of a category

Used to introduce specific examples of a general group or category mentioned before it.

I love music such as jazz and bossa nova.
Skills such as coding are in demand.
Cities such as São Paulo are vibrant.
